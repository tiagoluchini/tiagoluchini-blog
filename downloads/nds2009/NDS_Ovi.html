Nokia Developer Summit – The Ovi Platform, shifting onto services

If you haven't checked Ovi yet, you definitely should. Nokia's targets with Ovi are big and, one might say, quite megalomaniac. The master plan is to make Ovi a purely open service platform for a multitude of services including also services provided by partners.

The first gem of the Ovi package is Ovi-store. Ovi-store will be released still this year (late fall) first for a set of countries but soon to be rolled out globally. Ovi-store can be seen as the combination of Nokia's old Mosh community experience, the Download! application already sharing a large penetration base, Forum Nokia and Nokia's Widset service. Combined, these should provide enough background for Nokia to make Ovi-store really work.

Through Ovi-store, consumers will be able to discover, download, try and purchase apps or other contents for their mobile devices. The whole experience is supposed to be location-aware and to support viral behavior in a very community-driven, tribal way.

Developers will have the ability to self publish applications through publish.ovi.com and also sponsor their apps by hiring the so-called "spotlight" feature that will put their apps under special positioning during the end-user discovery process for a limited amount of time and to a specific public.

The money flow is also expected to be a great plus for the whole infra-structured being laid-down. Multiple payment modes will be supported by Ovi-store and this also includes a unique integrated approach with mobile operator billing systems. End-users will buy, pay through their operators globally and application providers will receive up to 70% of the revenue.

Even if not mentioned, it is clear that Nokia is trying to counter-attack Apple's app store currently supported on the iPhone.

But Ovi is not limited at all to Ovi-store. Nokia's desire is to make it a pure service platform through which apps developers can integrate social and location-based services that will engage end-users in the mobile experience. A very palpable first example is the plan for Nokia maps. Nokia maps are now located under Ovi (maps.ovi.com) and will expose a series of APIs later this year for application development. Nokia aims to create a healthy development ecosystem were social location services will flourish on top of maps on ovi. Other Ovi services will follow the same way paved by maps later and lead the Ovi concept into this service-centric mind.
