Internal newsletter 4/2009

Ixonos’ delegation reports from the Nokia Developer Summit

Nokia is changing. At least this was the message Nokia spared no efforts to communicate in its very first Nokia Developers Summit held in Monaco at the end of April. Ixonos delegates Tiago Luchini, Group Manager and Project Manager at Northern Finland Site, and Luis De Sousa Goncalves, Senior SW Designer from the Metropolitan area Site, were there to check out Nokia's future plans and report their findings here.

  • Qt Ubiquity

  • Webkit: Nokia's future is web

  • Flash increasingly more serious

  • The Ovi Platform, shifting onto services

  • Symbian Foundation and the future of S60

  • Maemo 5 Fremantle                                                                                       

  • Devices and others feelings

 
 All in all, Nokia is really trying to shift its image and attract developers to its platforms. Cost reduction and ease-of-development were the main points during most of the event. New languages, platform features, and tools are then the future for Nokia and consequentially for all those working with it or around it.
