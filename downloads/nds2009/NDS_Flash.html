Nokia Developer Summit – Flash increasingly more serious

Many have overseen Flash as a serious option for mobile apps due to its strong history to graphical and animation. Some have come to consider it simply a play-thing. Nokia is moving Flash to the "really-serious" category though. And they are not alone. Adobe is amazingly active into making Flash happen in the mobile platform: multi-touch support is being implemented, license costs have been removed, internal specs have been made public and, in simple worlds, Adobe is not afraid to "evangelize" businesses into using Flash for real in mobile devices.

Even though the latest Flash Lite 3.1 is supporting only ActionScript 2 and Flash 8-like applications the real break-through is behind the curtains: Flash Lite 3.1 is already a distributable and updatable component very much like its big brother today running on almost every single desktop computer on the planet. And, as we are at it, 300 million mobile devices currently carry Flash capabilities by default. The new generation of mobile flash players will not only be distributable but also support ActionScript 3 and Flash 10-like applications. This is expected to hit the market later this year.

Adobe and Nokia are also very much into porting Adobe's AIR technology into mobile devices. This can come as soon as next year and we got some inside info that this is at advanced development stages in Nokia. This will put Flash in a very comfortable and powerful position for mobile development.

This whole Flash horizon means great opportunities for ActionScript developers and those keen on Flash, Flex, AIR and related technologies.
