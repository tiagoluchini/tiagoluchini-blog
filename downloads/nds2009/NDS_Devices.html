Nokia Developer Summit – Devices and others feelings

Not surprisingly, Nokia did not focus the Summit too much on future devices. It was clear that the main message conveyed was about development platforms than hardware. However, the release of N97 was quickly highlighted. The main feature made available on this new device is the support of the new home screen platform and its Webkit widgets that can remain connect to the internet as well as rearranged by the user.

Some other interesting numbers regarding devices were made public. The first one was the number of Nokia devices in the market currently with embedded cameras: 500.000.000. This is more than any single camera-specific manufacturer. The second interesting number was the number of Nokia devices currently supporting Flash on the market: 300.000.000 phones. Good news for camera and Flash apps.

Microsoft's MySpace also presented their mobile strategy and mentioned that engagement from mobile users has proved to be higher than normal web users. Currently around 40% of all MySpace traffic comes from mobile clients. This is also expected to drastically grow during the next 5 years.

Creative UI design has also been highly praised as powerful means to achieve higher user engagement in mobile environments.  Apparently Nokia is finally aware that new UI concepts can be a good thing.

Location-based services are being called by Nokia as Social Location services as Nokia thrives to add the social context to the location context. As such, Nokia didn't spare hours of philosophical discussions about the subject.
